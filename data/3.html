Part 3

Three Axioms of MHC

  1. Higher order actions are defined in terms of two or more different lower order actions.
    • This puts the actions into a hierarchical relationship:
      • From set theory, a set and a member of a set cannot be at the same level.
  2. The higher order actions organize or transform the two or more lower order ones:
    • This is how the actions are combined to form the new action
  3. The organization is not arbitrary,
    • This makes the organization match the real world.

Instructions

  • Rule: If axiom 1 is violated, then axioms 2 and 3 are also violated.
  • If axiom 2 is violated, then axiom 3 is violated.
  • There will be examples.
  • What order does the example meet?
  • What next order does the example fail to meet?
  • What axioms are violated and preclude the example from reflecting the next higher order?
  • What are the elements (Variables, relations, systems, relations among systems) used in the example?
